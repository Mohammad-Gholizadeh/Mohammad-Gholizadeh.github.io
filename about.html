Biography

Mohammad Gholizadeh received his B.S. degree in Electrical Engineering from Jundi-Shapur University of Technology, Dezful, Iran, in 2016, and his M.S. degree in Electrical Engineering from Khajeh Nasir Toosi University of Technology (KNTU), Tehran, Iran, in 2023. He earned his Master's degree with a strong GPA of 17.09/20, and his thesis was evaluated with a high score of 18.99/20. Based on that work, He successfully published an article in the prestigious journal IEEE Transactions on Communications (TCOM), contributing to the field of telecommunications systems. His research interests include wireless communications, millimeter-wave networks, massive MIMO, cooperative networks (relay selection algorithms), beamforming, and machine learning.

Wireless Communications

Wireless communication has transformed how we connect, share, and access information.

Millimeter Wave Networks

Millimeter wave networks are paving the way for faster, more efficient wireless communication.

Multi-Hop Communications

Using intermediate nodes, multi-hop communication improves network resilience and expands coverage.

Massive MIMO

This technology enhances spectrum efficiency by employing multiple antennas at base stations.

Device-to-Device communications

This technology enables direct communication between devices, bypassing the need for central networks.

Machine Learning

Machine learning in wireless communication improves spectrum efficiency and network resource allocation.

Relay Selection Algorithms

These algorithms aim to improve network reliability and throughput by selecting the most suitable relays.

Stochastic Processes

These processes model the random nature of fading, noise, and channel conditions.

Numerical Optimization Methods

Numerical optimization techniques improve wireless communication by refining system parameters.
